Stubborn broken toshibas.

So, assuming you have a toshiba PDR-5 in the first place and also not a PDR-M5, then get an CR-123a and put it in the camera. the button of the battery will
rest on the bump of the battery cover.

The Problem

so your camera has a battery and you've pulled the power switch, but all you get is a red light. the screen displays nothing and pressing the shutter button provides
well, game over. but not really.
I'm not entirely sure if the screen is meant to not be very visible, but it will likely be displaying something still. It seems to be a non backlit Twisted Nematic
so you probably won't see much without direct light, and on these old toshibas, probably need a flashlight directly pointed at it. Set your camera to 'play' and
if the LED turns from red to orange/amber, then you're in luck. take a look at the screen and it will either say 'no card' (insert your Smart Media card, or insert it
the right way (they are not keyed/the slot is not keyed)), 'no image' (grats, the camera detects a memory card (max 16 MB, fat 12 format)) or you'll see your photos.

The Other problem

if you set it back to 'record' you'll notice that the LED turns back to red. and if yours is in the same condition as mine, it will stay red.
on the front of the camera by the lens is the macro/normal switch. with set to 'record' move the front switch to macro, wait, then move it back to normal.
if yours is like mine, the LED turns to orange. line up a photo, and press the shutter button. if the flash still works, it'll go off and take a picture. TADA!
its worth noting that the Red LED is also a flash charging light. if orange and you take a photo, it goes red, blinks, and goes orange.
I'm not sure if mine not responding until switching from macro and back is normal or not, but surely not.

notes

sometimes the screen might actively display what the lens sees. I'm not sure how to get that to happen. one of the small round buttons activates this I think
expect lots of ghosting on that display.
often, the openable back/PC Card interface is broken and the plastic is cracked. I believe mine has done so and now the buttons on the same face as the screen don't work
especially the d-pad. this means that you cannot adjust the settings in the setting menu nor scroll to view other photos on 'play' mode.
if you somehow have a working PC Card back thing, and put it through an adaptor slot on a PC, it won't work without the drivers probably. no one has put the driver online yet
but I think I might have the opportunity to get it and do so.
sometimes you might get a PDR-5 without the wrist strap. GET A WRIST STRAP. the plastic has not aged well on these and the plastics are very creaky on mine.
these are coming up to be 28b years old in a few months as of writing, so it will only worsen from here. one day they will all fall apart in hand.
if you're looking for replacment straps, get one of those third party Wii gamepad straps. they work nice and are cheap and good quality.
the purpose of a wrist strap is to prevent drops to the camera, but in this case doubly so. drop them and you may damage electronics, but almost definitely crack apart
the now brittle plastics.

Conclusion

uh, I dunno. hope this helped. I got mine as broken for about 10 USD but it ust needed a battery and me to figure out what exactly was going on,
that I have now written down because I needed this info back when I first got it.
